# Reflection
**Topic 2 — New Skills Required in the AI Era**

When I began this research, my expectation was fairly simple: AI is powerful, it will automate a lot of coding work, and software engineers will need to learn how to use AI tools. After actually reading through industry reports, developer surveys, and academic research, I found that the reality is both more nuanced and more urgent than I initially expected.

**What surprised me most** was the trust problem. I expected developers to enthusiastically embrace AI tools as productivity multipliers. What the data actually shows — from Stack Overflow's 2025 survey — is that more developers actively *distrust* AI output (46%) than trust it (33%). Experienced developers are the most skeptical. This is counterintuitive. The more senior you are, the more you see AI as a tool that needs supervision, not a partner you can rely on. This tells me that critical thinking and code review are not soft skills to mention on a CV — they are now core technical responsibilities. I had not fully appreciated how much human judgment is still required at every step.
